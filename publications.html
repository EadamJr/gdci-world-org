Publications

Reports, research papers, policy briefs, opinion articles, and analysis produced by GDCI fellows and affiliated scholars on West–East civilizational dialogue, intercultural relations, and Middle East–Global North engagement.

Policy Brief

Why Dialogue is Not Compromise: Identity, Diplomacy, and the Plural World

Dr. Layla Al-Amin · May 2026 · Dialogue & Peacebuilding

Reframing international negotiation as an instrument of mutual identity-recognition rather than concession, drawing on Islamic jurisprudence and Western liberal theory in parallel. The brief argues that productive diplomacy between the Global North and Middle East requires a departure from zero-sum frameworks toward dialogue as an epistemological practice.

Research Paper

At the Margins of Empires: What History Teaches at Civilizational Borders

Prof. Erik Sandström · June 2026 · Heritage & History

A comparative analysis of historical contact zones — from Andalusia to the Levant Crusader states — where shared legal and cultural frameworks formed across religious-civilizational lines. The paper draws on archival sources in Arabic, Latin, and Hebrew to reconstruct mechanisms of coexistence that inform contemporary cross-civilizational governance.

Report

Governance Across Traditions: Three Models of Legitimate Authority

GDCI Research Team · April 2026 · Governance & Economy

An institutional comparison of state legitimacy and constitutionalism across Chinese Confucian, classical Islamic, and European liberal models. The report proposes a cross-traditional framework for multilateral cooperation that moves beyond Western-centric assumptions in international law and governance design.

Opinion

The Mirror Problem: How Western Media Shapes Arab Self-Perception

Nadia Osman · March 2026 · Media & Narratives

An essay examining the structural feedback loops by which Western representations of the Middle East shape — and distort — political discourse within Arab societies themselves. The author argues for media-literacy programmes specifically designed to interrupt these feedback mechanisms as a precondition for genuine dialogue.

Analysis

Youth as Mediators: A Field Assessment of Cross-Cultural Youth Dialogue Programmes

Dr. Amir Hassan & Dr. Sophie Vidal · February 2026 · Youth & Education

A systematic review of 14 structured youth dialogue programmes operating between European and MENA participants from 2020–2025. The analysis identifies success conditions, failure modes, and scaling pathways for youth-centred interventions in West–East relations, drawing on survey data from over 600 young participants.
