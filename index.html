Global Dialogue Civilization Initiative

Where West Meets East — and Listens.

An independent initiative facilitating, supporting, researching, and advocating for structured dialogue between the Global North and the Middle East — across academia, policy, faith, and civil society.

Explore Our Research About GDCI
Our Mission
"Dialogue is not agreement. It is the willingness to be changed by understanding another civilization on its own terms."

GDCI brings together academics, researchers, policymakers, youth leaders, religious figures, and civil society advocates from the Global North and the Arab and Islamic world to build mutual understanding through rigorous, respectful, and sustained engagement.

What GDCI Does

Four Pillars of Engagement

GDCI operates across four interconnected fields of action that transform dialogue from aspiration into structured practice.

Facilitate

Convening structured forums, roundtables, and exchanges between Western and Middle Eastern voices — creating conditions where authentic, equitable dialogue can take root.

Support

Providing fellowships, mentorship, and institutional backing to scholars, youth, and practitioners doing the difficult work of cross-civilizational engagement.

Research

Producing independent, peer-reviewed research on the historical, political, cultural, and religious dimensions of West–East relations to ground dialogue in evidence.

Advocate

Championing policies and institutions that reduce hostility, foster mutual recognition, and create lasting structural conditions for civilizational cooperation.

Our Community

Who Makes Up GDCI

GDCI is a pluralist community of practice. Our members share a commitment to rigorous, respectful, and sustained engagement across civilizational divides.

🎓

Academics & Researchers

Scholars from universities and think-tanks in Europe, North America, and the MENA region producing rigorous comparative research.

🌍

Youth Leaders

Young professionals and emerging voices from both sides of the dialogue who represent the next generation of bridge-builders.

🕌

Religious Figures

Scholars of Islamic thought, Christian theologians, and interfaith practitioners committed to dialogue that neither compromises nor avoids difference.

⚖️

Policy Advocates

Diplomats, NGO leaders, and civil-society organisations working to translate dialogue into durable institutional change.

📰

Media & Journalists

Reporters and media scholars from both regions whose coverage shapes the narrative environment in which dialogue succeeds or fails.

🏛️

Civil Society

Community organisations and civic networks that embed dialogue in local practice and extend its reach beyond elite forums.

Global Presence

Conferences We've Participated In

GDCI actively engages in major international forums where West–East dialogue and civilizational cooperation are advanced on the global stage.

November 2024 — Cascais, Portugal

10th UNAOC Global Forum — "United in Peace: Restoring Trust, Reshaping the Future"

The flagship event of the United Nations Alliance of Civilizations, convening political leaders, religious actors, academia, youth, and civil society from across the globe. GDCI fellows participated in panels on intercultural dialogue and Western–Islamic world relations.

Learn more →
May 2024 — Baku, Azerbaijan

6th World Forum on Intercultural Dialogue — "Dialogue for Peace and Global Security"

Co-organised by Azerbaijan, UNESCO, the UN Alliance of Civilizations, the Council of Europe, and ICESCO. GDCI presented findings on religious-civic dialogue mechanisms in MENA contexts.

Learn more →
February 2025 — Abu Dhabi, UAE

International Dialogue of Civilizations and Tolerance Conference (2nd Edition)

Hosted under UAE leadership and organised by the Emirates Scholar Center for Research and Studies. Experts from academia, politics, and civil society examined the core questions of civilizational dialogue — particularly tensions between the Arab world and the West.

Learn more →
June 2023 — Marrakesh, Morocco

Parliamentary Conference on Interfaith Dialogue — "Working Together for Our Common Future"

Organised by the Inter-Parliamentary Union and the Parliament of Morocco. GDCI contributed policy recommendations on institutional frameworks for West–Arab dialogue to the first-ever parliamentary conference on interfaith dialogue.

Learn more →
April 2024 — UNESCO HQ, Paris

UNESCO Courier Forum — "Intercultural Dialogue and Mutual Learning: Shared Horizons"

A high-level convening at UNESCO House exploring cross-civilizational exchange from Silk Road history to contemporary digital public spheres. GDCI researchers presented a comparative study of Western and Middle Eastern media ecosystems and their role in widening or bridging civilizational gaps.
